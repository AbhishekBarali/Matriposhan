Pregnancy is meant to be a time of hope, strength and care

40%+ of Pregnant
Women Suffer
from Anemia

▶

And in South Asia the burden is even bigger

A condition that increases the risk of:

⚠ Maternal mortality
⚠ Severe fatigue
⚠ Perinatal mortality
⚠ Premature birth
⚠ Postpartum haemorrhage
⚠ Low birth weight in newborns

What makes this crisis even more devastating is that we already have the knowledge to prevent it.

What we lack is accessible, affordable, and effective delivery of maternal nutrition, especially for women in low-resource communities.

Preventable

Most cases of maternal anemia can be prevented with proper nutrition.

Predictable

We know exactly which women are most at risk

Persistent

Yet the crisis continues, claiming lives and futures that could have been saved

Our visit

Spoke with healthcare providers

To understand this challenge beyond statistics, we visited hospitals and spoke with healthcare providers.

Ground Reality

Anemia is often detected late, nutrition support is inconsistent, and many women simply don't have access to practical solutions.
