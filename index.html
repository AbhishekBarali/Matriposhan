Real Stories

Documented individuals in Nepal — the women behind the statistics

Story 01

Parvati

Kailali, Nepal
Bleeding after a home birth, then an anaemia diagnosis
What happened

Parvati (30) had already faced devastating loss — a baby who died at 3 months, then a miscarriage. In a later pregnancy she became very weak, delivered at home, and kept bleeding until she fainted. A health worker urged urgent hospital care; she regained consciousness three days later and learned she had survived postpartum haemorrhage with vitamin A deficiency and anaemia.

Why it mattered

This is the brutal anaemia-bleeding loop: when you start pregnancy with low iron, even "normal" blood loss can become dangerous.

What changed

She joined a mothers' group and learned safe-motherhood practices. In her next pregnancy, she improved her diet (vegetables, fruits, beans), took iron tablets, did checkups, and delivered in a hospital with a healthy baby.

Where Matriposhan fits: Matriposhan prevents the "weak → home birth → heavy bleeding → collapse" pathway by bundling iron-rich foods + supplements + simple family-shared guidance before the crisis point.

Story 02

Elina Gauchan

Kathmandu, Nepal
Teenage pregnancy, dizziness/blackouts, transfusion after delivery
What happened

Elina (18) came from Okhaldhunga to Kathmandu for work and became pregnant soon after marriage. She didn't recognise anaemia early and had limited prenatal checkups. During pregnancy she experienced dizziness and blackouts; she reached a hospital only around her sixth month. At delivery, she was diagnosed as anaemic, had continued bleeding, and received a blood transfusion. Weeks later she was still anaemic, taking iron-folic acid and trying to eat more greens.

Why it mattered

Her story highlights three common failure points: Low awareness ("I didn't know what this was"), Late detection, and Food reality — eating whatever is in the kitchen, heavy workload, and low control over diet.

Where Matriposhan fits: Matriposhan acts earlier than a hospital-only solution — building daily habits (food + supplement adherence) so anaemia doesn't wait until delivery to be discovered.

Story 03

Nanda Keshari Maharjan

Khokana, Lalitpur, Nepal
Severe anaemia improved when it was caught early
What happened

Nanda Keshari had severe anaemia in both pregnancies. In her first pregnancy she felt weak and lifeless and didn't know what to do. In her second pregnancy, anaemia was identified during an initial prenatal visit, and she received iron tablets plus diet advice — and she did not have the same weakness and fatigue.

Why it mattered

Same woman, different outcome — because of early contact + practical guidance. This is proof that early intervention works.

Where Matriposhan fits: Matriposhan makes "early + practical" support the default, not the lucky exception — especially where first-time mothers don't know what symptoms mean.
